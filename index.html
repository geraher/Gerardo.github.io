Bio

I was born April 26 1988 in Culiacan, Sinaloa. My father is a doctor and my mother is a schoolteacher. I have 2 brothers, Luis who is a mechanical engineer & Alex who is an economist.
I spent all my childhood until adulthood in Culiacan, I have only peaceful memories in contrast into what is portrayed in the news. Our parents raised us in a separate world which I think help us grew secure of ourselves and considering family as the most important.
In 2006, I moved to Monterrey to pursuit my architectural studies in a house with my two brothers. There I found great freedom to pursuit my interests like music. I was part of the folk group Raices and traveled the world performing music during the summers, which is my most memorable experience during that period.
I also participated with a multidisciplinary team in the Solar Decathlon edition of 2010, with a development period of 2 years. Unfortunately, a few months before the competition, our school could not gather the funds to start construction and we had to withdraw. Beside that disappointment, the experience still was eye opening since I learned that even a small house could integrate so much complexity. One must also has to be a good leader to guarantee the completion of any ambitious project and provide useful insights for the team, which is also, why I keep preparing myself constantly. Finally, in 2011 I finished my architectural studies and was happy to receive a DEXFI award from my institution for my participation in extracurricular activities.
After graduation, I worked as an architect; first in a design studio, then in a construction company. During that time, I felt that I was not learning what I wanted to learn about construction, so I left my job and continue my studies; now as a civil engineer.
I studied in U ERRE while working half time; I was lucky since thanks to a friend I found a half time job as an environmental consultant something I wanted to try since my experience in the Solar Decathlon competition. It was difficult to manage both, but I think I organized quite well since I graduated with the best grades of my faculty class and became a LEED Green Associate.
I worked full time in Kalos as a draftsman for industrial project towards LEED certification. I found it fulfilling since I had the opportunity to work with the latest software technology like Revit to create construction drawings and steel detailing for fabrication of metal structures.
